Every figure in CURB traces to the single provided enforcement dataset. No external data, no scraping, no invented ground truth. Where a number is an index rather than a measurement, we say so.

How we rank — the obstruction-impact index

Each violation is scored by severity (where it happens — main road, footpath, bus stop) times the vehicle footprint (road space a vehicle type occupies, in passenger-car units). Summed onto a ~55 m grid, this gives each spot an impact index.

cell_impact = Σ ( severity × vehicle_footprint ) → normalised to 0–100

It is a prioritisation index, not measured or predicted traffic. A spot scoring 100 carries the most obstruction burden in the data — it is not "100% congested."

Root cause & prescription

The vehicle mix at a spot implies its cause: heavy goods → a delivery problem (loading bay); two-wheeler dominance → commuter overflow (park-and-ride); autos → hire/transit demand (stand); safety-critical locations → safety risk (bollards). Each cause maps to a differentiated fix rather than a generic "send a patrol."

Persistence

Spots are tiered by how many distinct days they recur: Chronic (standing posts), Frequent, Occasional. This separates a permanent problem from an occasional one.

Trends

Citywide volume is flat across the window (second/first-half ratio ≈ 1.01), so a spot rising faster than the city is genuinely emerging. This is a retrospective trend over ~5 months — not a forecast. Hour-of-day is unreliable in this dataset and is not used; only day/week granularity is.

Repeat offenders

These are repeat detections, not proven offences — a vehicle caught often may simply park where enforcement is heavy. Vehicle IDs are anonymised: aggregate targeting, never owner identification. A location where the same vehicles return points to a structural fix; many different vehicles points to enforcement presence.

Data & compliance

Provided dataset only. The assistant's language model phrases results already computed from that data — it never adds outside facts, retrieves, or browses. Severity and footprint weights are transparent, self-defined constants (a formula, not external data) and are listed in the source repository.
